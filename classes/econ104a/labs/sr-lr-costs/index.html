Short-Run & Long-Run Cost Curves

With production function Q = √K · √L, the long-run average cost is constant at 2√(wr). Each short-run curve (fixed K̄) is U-shaped and tangent to LRATC exactly at the quantity where K̄ is the long-run optimal capital. Drag the sliders to explore the envelope relationship.

At Q* = K̄·√(r/w), the chosen K̄ is exactly the long-run optimal capital for that output level. Because K*(Q*) = Q*·√(w/r) = K̄, the firm's short-run and long-run costs coincide: SATC(Q*) = LRATC = 2√(wr). Everywhere else, SATC > LRATC.
