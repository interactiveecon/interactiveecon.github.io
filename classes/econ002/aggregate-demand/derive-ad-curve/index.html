Deriving Aggregate Demand

See how a change in the price level shifts the FR curve, changes equilibrium output, and traces out the AD curve — one point at a time.

How this app works
  1. The economy starts at price level \(P_1\) (baseline). The IS–FR diagram shows equilibrium at point A.
  2. Click \(P_2\) — Higher Price Level. Watch the FR curve shift up because the Fed raises rates when prices rise. Notice output falls to \(Y_2\). Point B is plotted on the AD diagram below.
  3. Click \(P_3\) — Lower Price Level. The FR curve shifts down, output rises to \(Y_3\). Point C is plotted.
  4. Once all three points are plotted, the AD curve connects them — revealing why it is downward sloping.

Key idea: The AD curve is not like a regular supply/demand curve. Each point on it is a full IS–FR equilibrium. Moving along AD means the price level changes and the FR curve shifts in response.
