Marginal Product and Diminishing Marginal Product

This lab uses discrete marginal products: MPL = Y(K,L+1) − Y(K,L) and MPK = Y(K+1,L) − Y(K,L). The graphs show the two output points and the implied change in output.

Curves shift when A changes or when the “held-fixed” input changes. The previous curve remains faintly in the background.

What to look for

Make the graphs do the teaching.

Diminishing MPL: holding K fixed, the vertical gap between \(Y(K,L)\) and \(Y(K,L+1)\) shrinks as L gets larger.

Diminishing MPK: holding L fixed, the vertical gap between \(Y(K,L)\) and \(Y(K+1,L)\) shrinks as K gets larger.
Discrete marginal product: we are using “+1” changes, not derivatives. That’s why the marked points and the displayed ΔY matter.
