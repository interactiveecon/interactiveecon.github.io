Marginal Product and Diminishing Marginal Product

Graph-first lab using discrete marginal products: MPL = Y(K,L+1) − Y(K,L) and MPK = Y(K+1,L) − Y(K,L). Baseline curves stay fixed in grey.

• Holding K fixed, the vertical gap between Y(K,L) and Y(K,L+1) shrinks as L rises (diminishing MPL).
• Holding L fixed, the vertical gap between Y(K,L) and Y(K+1,L) shrinks as K rises (diminishing MPK).
• Grey line = baseline (never changes). Colored line = your current settings.

Sliders

A, K, and L move in steps of 1. K and L run from 1 to 10.

Parameters
The production function used internally is stylized to make concavity visually obvious: \(Y = A\sqrt{K}\sqrt{L}\).
