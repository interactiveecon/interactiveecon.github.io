Unemployment Categories and Flows

Sort people into Employed, Unemployed, or Not in the Labor Force. Once you are done sorting, click check catagories to make sure you have done it correctly. If they are all correct, click Use these counts for more cards to lock in your answers. You can then use the Move Cards section. In this section you will be given four scenarios for people to move across the catagories (you can get different ones by selecting Reroll Move Cards). Before selecting one of the scenerios, use the Choose a prediction to predict what you think will happen to the unemployment rate (u) and labor force participation rate (LFPR). Then select your move and see what happens.

Key definitions
  • Labor force: \(LF = E + U\)
  • Unemployment rate: \(u = \dfrac{U}{E+U}\)
  • LFPR: \(LFPR = \dfrac{E+U}{E+U+N}\)
  • Population (in this lab): \(Pop = E+U+N\)

Classify the cards

Start in the staging pile. Drag each card to the correct category.

Staging pile

Unsorted cards. Drag them into E, U, or N.

Employed (E)

Worked for pay (or in a family business) during the reference period.

Unemployed (U)

Not working, available, and actively searching (or on temporary layoff).

Not in the labor force (N)

Not working and not actively searching (students, retirees, discouraged, etc.).
