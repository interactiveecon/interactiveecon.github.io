Types of Unemployment

Sort scenario cards into frictional, structural, or cyclical. Then see how cyclical unemployment changes over the business cycle and how strong expansions can reduce frictional unemployment.

Key ideas
  • Frictional: search/matching (can shrink in strong expansions)
  • Structural: mismatch (held fixed in this app)
  • Cyclical: weak demand in recessions (zero in expansions)
  • Neutral: growth = 2% → cyclical = 0 and frictional returns to baseline
How this app works
  • Sort cards into Frictional, Structural, or Cyclical.
  • The timeline starts at neutral growth: growth = 2.0% in every quarter.
  • Labor force (LF) is fixed for the round. Employment adjusts as E = LF − U.
  • Structural stays fixed at its baseline in every quarter.
  • Recession (growth < 2%): frictional returns to baseline and cyclical unemployment rises. Cyclical is distributed unevenly across cyclical cards (so they don’t all move by the same amount).
  • Expansion (growth > 2%): cyclical is set to 0. If the expansion is strong, frictional falls below baseline. As growth moves back toward 2%, frictional moves back toward baseline.
  • Frictional cards display baseline vs current when they’re in the frictional bucket.
  • The unemployment-rate chart shows u and u*; the highlighted vertical gap is labeled cyclical unemployment.
