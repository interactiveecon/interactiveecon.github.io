Types of Unemployment

Sort scenario cards into frictional, structural, or cyclical. Then see how cyclical unemployment moves with the business cycle.

Key ideas
  • Frictional: search/matching
  • Structural: mismatch (skills/location/industry changes)
  • Cyclical: weak demand in recessions
  • Natural rate: \(u^* = u_f + u_s\)
  • Cyclical rate: \(u-u^*\)

How cyclical unemployment is generated
We map output gap to a cyclical unemployment rate using a simple rule:
cyclical rate = max(0, κ · (−gap))
Then we convert that rate into a cyclical unemployed count:
C_target = round(LF · cyclical rate)

The app adjusts the people counts on cards in the Cyclical bucket so they sum to C_target.
