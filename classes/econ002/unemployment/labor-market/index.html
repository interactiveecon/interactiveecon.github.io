Labor Market: Cyclical vs Structural Unemployment

Use the labor market graph to see how unemployment can arise from (i) sticky nominal wages + labor demand shocks (cyclical), or (ii) a real-wage rigidity (structural).

Key relationships
  • Axes: real wage \(w = W/P\) vs employment \(L\)
  • Labor demand: \(w = a - bL\)
  • Labor supply: \(w = c + dL\)
  • If the actual real wage is above equilibrium, employment is demand-determined and unemployment is \(L_s - L_d\).

Graph and controls

Grey = baseline. Blue = current. Unemployment is shaded when it exists.

Cyclical mechanism: sticky nominal wage \(W\).
When a demand shock hits, \(W\) does not adjust quickly, so \(w=W/P\) can be “too high.”
Structural mechanism: real-wage floor \( \bar{w} \).
The real wage cannot fall below \( \bar{w} \) (e.g., minimum wage / unions / efficiency wages).
