Labor Market: Cyclical vs Structural Unemployment

Start in equilibrium. Then compare unemployment from a negative labor demand shock with (i) cyclical unemployment (downward-sticky real wage) versus (ii) structural unemployment (real-wage floor).

How to use this
  • Pick cyclical unemployment or structural unemployment.
  • Use demand shock buttons to shift labor demand.
  • Cyclical: after a negative shock, the real wage doesn't fall (sticky downward), so unemployment appears. After a positive shock, the real wage rises.
  • Structural: the real wage cannot fall below \(\bar w\).
  • Unemployment is the gap between labor supplied and labor demanded at the actual wage.

Graph and controls

Grey dashed = baseline. Blue = current. Orange line = actual real wage. Unemployment is shaded when it exists.
