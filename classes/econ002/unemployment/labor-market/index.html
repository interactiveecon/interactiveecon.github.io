Labor Market: Cyclical vs Structural Unemployment

Start in equilibrium. Then compare unemployment from a negative labor demand shock with (i) a sticky real wage (cyclical) versus (ii) a real-wage floor (structural).

How to use this
  • Pick cyclical unemployment or structural unemployment.
  • Use demand shock buttons to shift labor demand.
  • Cyclical: the real wage is downward sticky (won’t fall after a negative shock) but can rise after a positive shock.
  • Structural: the real wage is pinned by a floor \( \bar w \).
  • If the actual real wage is above equilibrium, employment is demand-determined and unemployment is \(L_s - L_d\).

Graph and controls

Grey dashed = baseline. Blue = current. Orange line = actual real wage. Unemployment is shaded when it exists.

Cyclical unemployment
Demand shocks move labor demand. After a negative shock, the real wage doesn’t fall (sticky downward), so unemployment appears. After a positive shock, the real wage rises.
Structural unemployment
A real-wage rigidity pins the wage at a floor \( \bar w \). If \( \bar w \) is above equilibrium, unemployment persists.
