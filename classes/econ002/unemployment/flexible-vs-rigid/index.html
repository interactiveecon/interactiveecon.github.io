Flexible vs. Sticky Wages

How does the labor market respond to a shock when wages can adjust freely versus when they are stuck? Step through the adjustment process in both worlds and see why sticky wages produce unemployment.

How this app works
  1. Choose a shock type — a recession (labor demand falls) or a boom (labor demand rises).
  2. Set the shock size with the slider.
  3. Click Next Step to walk through the adjustment one stage at a time, or Show All to see the full outcome immediately.
  4. Compare the left panel (flexible wages) with the right panel (sticky wages) at each step.

Key contrast: With flexible wages, the market clears at a lower wage — employment falls but there is no unemployment. With sticky wages, the real wage stays at \(W/P^*\), employment falls further, and a gap opens between workers who want jobs and jobs available — that gap is cyclical unemployment.
