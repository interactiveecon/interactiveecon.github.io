Multiplier Explorer

See how a change in \(G\) or \(T\) ripples through spending rounds to produce a total change in output larger than the initial policy. Adjust the MPC and policy size to build intuition for \(\frac{1}{1-MPC}\) and \(\frac{-MPC}{1-MPC}\).

How this app works
  1. Choose a policy type: ΔG (government purchases), ΔT (taxes), or Balanced Budget (equal ΔG and ΔT).
  2. Set the MPC and policy size using the sliders.
  3. The Planned Expenditure diagram shows the PE line shifting and the new equilibrium.
  4. The Spending Rounds chart breaks the total ΔY into successive waves of induced consumption — so you can see exactly why output changes by more than the initial policy.
  5. The rounds table shows each wave numerically.

Key contrasts to notice: (1) A \(\Delta G\) of 100 raises output by more than a \(\Delta T\) of −100 of the same size — why? (2) What happens to the multiplier as MPC → 1? (3) For the balanced budget, why does output rise by exactly \(\Delta G\)?
