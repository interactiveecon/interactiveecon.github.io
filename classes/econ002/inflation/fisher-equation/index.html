Fisher Equation & Inflation: Who Wins and Who Loses?

Explore how nominal interest, expected inflation, and unexpected inflation affect real interest rates and redistribute wealth between borrowers and lenders.

How this works
  • Fisher equation (approx.): \(\;r^{e} \approx i - \pi^{e}\), where \(i\) is the nominal interest rate and \(\pi^{e}\) is expected inflation.
  • Realized real return: \(\;r^{real} \approx i - \pi\), where \(\pi\) is actual inflation.
  • Expected vs unexpected inflation: if \(\pi = \pi^{e}\), inflation is fully expected (no surprise). if \(\pi \ne \pi^{e}\), inflation is unexpected and it redistributes between borrowers and lenders.
  • Use the sliders to set \(i\), \(\pi^{e}\), and \(\pi\). Then see who wins for a loan and for a savings deposit.
