CPI Basket Builder

Load a random basket of goods and compute the CPI (base year = 100) and inflation.

How this app works
  1. Click Load example to draw 3 random goods and randomize the basket and prices.
  2. The Basket column is the fixed consumption basket used to compute CPI (it stays fixed across years).
  3. Edit prices for Year 1, Year 2, and Year 3. The results and charts update automatically.
  4. How CPI is computed: Year 1 is the base year (\(\text{CPI}_1=100\)). For \(t=2,3\), \(\text{CPI}_t = 100 \cdot \dfrac{\text{Cost}_t}{\text{Cost}_1}\), where \(\text{Cost}_t=\sum_i P_{i,t}\cdot \text{Basket}_i\).
  5. How inflation is computed: \(\pi_{1\to2}=\dfrac{\text{CPI}_2-\text{CPI}_1}{\text{CPI}_1}\) and \(\pi_{2\to3}=\dfrac{\text{CPI}_3-\text{CPI}_2}{\text{CPI}_2}\).
  6. Charts: dashed lines show the baseline CPI/inflation for the current basket. Solid lines show the current values after your edits.
  7. When the baseline resets: if you add/remove goods or change a basket quantity, the baseline updates to match the new basket using the prices currently shown.
  8. Check Yourself (MCQs): questions are generated from your current basket/prices. After you change prices, try answering again to see how the logic changes.
  9. Reset to baseline values (MCQ button): restores prices back to the dashed-line baseline so you can test different “what if” changes from the same starting point.
  10. Tip: the number-input arrows change prices by 1% of the current price (min $0.01).
