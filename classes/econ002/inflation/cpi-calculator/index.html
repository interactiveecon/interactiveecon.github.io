CPI Basket Builder

Load a random basket of goods and compute the CPI (base year = 100) and inflation.

How this app works
  1. Click Load example to draw 3 random goods and randomize the basket and prices.
  2. The Basket column is the fixed consumption basket used to compute CPI (it stays fixed across years).
  3. Enter or edit prices for Year 1, Year 2, and Year 3.
  4. The app computes basket costs each year: \(\;\sum_i P_{i,t}\cdot \text{Basket}_i\).
  5. The CPI uses Year 1 as the base year: \(\text{CPI}_1=100\) and \(\text{CPI}_t = 100 \cdot \dfrac{\text{Cost}_t}{\text{Cost}_1}\).
  6. Inflation is the one-year percent change in CPI: \(\pi_{1\to2}=\dfrac{\text{CPI}_2-\text{CPI}_1}{\text{CPI}_1}\), \(\pi_{2\to3}=\dfrac{\text{CPI}_3-\text{CPI}_2}{\text{CPI}_2}\).
  7. Baseline vs current: the dashed lines on the charts show the original CPI/inflation for the current basket. Changing prices updates the solid line only. Adding/removing goods (or changing the basket) resets the dashed baseline.
  8. Tip: the number-input arrows change prices by 1% of the current price (min $0.01).
