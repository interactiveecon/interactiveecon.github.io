CPI vs GDP Deflator (with PCE notes)

Sort scenarios into buckets. Then simulate a shock and compare how CPI inflation, GDP deflator inflation, and PCE inflation respond.

How this works
  • CPI: prices faced by consumers (includes imports; excludes most investment goods).
  • GDP deflator: prices of domestic production (includes investment, government purchases, exports; excludes imports).
  • PCE (notes in explanations): also consumer-focused, but uses different weights and allows more substitution (often smoother than CPI).
