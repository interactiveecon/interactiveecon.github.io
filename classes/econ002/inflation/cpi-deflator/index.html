Inflation Measures Lab

Sort scenarios by whether they change measured inflation in CPI, PCE, and/or the GDP deflator. Then simulate a shock and compare the three inflation series.

How to use this app
  1. Click New Round. A random set of cards appears in Staging.
  2. Drag each card into the correct bucket based on: “Does this scenario change measured inflation in that index?”
  3. Bucket meanings:
    • CPI & PCE: changes consumer inflation measures (imports are a classic example).
    • PCE & GDP Deflator: changes flexible/chain-weighted measures (substitution/composition effects), but not CPI.
    • GDP Deflator only: changes domestic-production inflation (investment, government purchases, exports), but not consumer indexes.
    • All: broad domestic consumer prices (like haircuts/restaurants) change all three.
    • None: does not directly change measured inflation (asset prices, transfers, resale prices of existing assets).
  4. Click Check to see results and brief explanations.
  5. In Shock simulation, click a shock to see 6-quarter inflation paths for CPI, PCE, and GDP deflator, then answer the 2 MCQs.
