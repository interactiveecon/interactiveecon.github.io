Fed Rule: Movement vs Shift

Practice: what causes a movement along the Fed rule vs a shift of the rule.

How this app works

The graph shows a Fed rule in \((Y,r)\) space: \(Y\) on the horizontal axis, \(r\) on the vertical axis. You will see a baseline point \((Y_1,r_1)\).

  • Movement along: when output \(Y\) changes, you move to a different point on the same line.
  • Shift: when something other than output changes the Fed’s stance (price level/inflation pressure, uncertainty/financial conditions), the whole line shifts up or down.
  1. Click New Scenario to get a short “news brief.”
  2. Make predictions (movement vs shift; what happens to \(r\) and \(Y\)) and click Check.
  3. Then use the sliders for \(Y\), \(P\), and \(Z\) to implement what you think changed.
  4. When you match the scenario, the graph labels \(Y_2\) and \(r_2\) and draws an arrow showing the change.

Tip: This lab is about classifying changes. The “Why?” button explains the intuition using the scenario story.
