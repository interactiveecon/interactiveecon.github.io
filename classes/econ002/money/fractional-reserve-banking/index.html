T-Accounts & Money Creation

See how an initial reserve injection creates deposits (money) through bank lending. The T-account is a balance sheet: Assets = Liabilities each step.

Instructions

Goal

Build intuition for how reserves and the required reserve ratio determine how much deposit money the banking system can create.

How to use the app

  1. Click New Scenario. The app draws:
    • An initial reserve injection \(\Delta R\)
    • A required reserve ratio \(rr\) (this becomes the minimum value of the slider)
  2. Click Step to move through the chain of banks:
    • A new deposit arrives at the current bank.
    • The bank holds a fraction \(rr\) of that deposit as reserves.
    • The rest becomes a new loan.
    • That loan is deposited at the next bank in the next step.
  3. Use Run to completion to iterate until the next loan is tiny (or until the max number of steps).
  4. Use Reset to restart the steps for the same scenario.

How to read what you see

  • Deposit split bar: shows how each deposit is split into reserves held and a new loan.
  • Next step preview (ghost bar): shows what the next bank will receive as a deposit (equal to the current loan), and how it will split.
  • T-account (balance sheet):
    • Assets: Reserves and Loans
    • Liabilities: Deposits
    • It always satisfies Assets = Liabilities.
  • Totals: the running totals show system-wide deposits (money), loans, and reserves held.

What to try

  • Move the reserve ratio slider upward. Higher \(rr\) means each bank holds more reserves and lends less, so the deposit expansion process gets smaller.
  • Compare the theoretical multiplier \(m=\frac{1}{rr}\) to the implied multiplier \(m=\frac{M}{\Delta R}\) shown in the totals.

This is a simplified model designed for intuition: loans become deposits and the required reserve ratio governs how much each bank can lend out of new deposits.
