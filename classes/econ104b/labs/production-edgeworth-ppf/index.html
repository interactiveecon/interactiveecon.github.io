ECON 104B — General Equilibrium

Production Edgeworth Box & PPF Mapper

Cereal, Inc. and Pancake, Inc. share 20 units of labor and 12 units of capital. Drag the input allocation point inside the production Edgeworth box to see how each firm's isoquants shift, when input efficiency is achieved, and how the allocation maps onto the production possibilities frontier.

How to use this lab
  1. Cereal Inc.'s origin is the bottom-left corner of the box; Pancake Inc.'s origin is the top-right corner.
  2. Blue isoquants are Cereal Inc.'s; amber isoquants are Pancake, Inc.'s. Each isoquant traces input combinations that yield the same output for that firm.
  3. An allocation is input-efficient when the two firms' isoquants are tangent — that is, the marginal rates of technical substitution (MRTS) are equal. Inefficient points lie inside the production possibilities frontier on the right panel.
  4. Questions unlock in order. Press New Problem at the bottom of the page to regenerate the production weights and starting allocation at any time.
