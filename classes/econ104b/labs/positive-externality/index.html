ECON 104B — Externalities

Positive Externality & Subsidy

A producer creates a positive externality through an external benefit. Derive the private and socially optimal outcomes, measure the resulting deadweight loss, and find the per-unit subsidy that restores efficiency.

Instructions

In this program you are given the opportunity to practice solving for socially optimal production, deadweight loss, and optimal subsidies when the production of a good generates an external benefit. You will be given the private inverse demand function, the total cost of the firm for producing Q units of the good, and the total external benefit to society from Q units of production.

You will be asked to answer the following questions:

  1. Find the optimal amount for the producer to sell. This is the private market outcome where price equals marginal cost.
  2. Find the socially optimal amount for the producer to sell. This accounts for the external benefit that others receive from production.
  3. Find the deadweight loss due to the fact that the producer will voluntarily produce less than the socially optimal amount.
  4. Find the optimal subsidy that the government should provide to the producer in order to get them to produce the socially optimal amount.

For each step, enter your answer and click Check Answer. If your answer is incorrect, hints and the worked solution are available.
